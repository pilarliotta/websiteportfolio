Data Portrait

Generative portrait created from personal data. An exploration of identity, data privacy, and the intersection of technology and art.

Reflection

For this data portrait, I tracked every song I listened to for a week. I noticed that even though I listened to tons of music, I kept returning to the same three songs:

Champagne Coast — 17 plays (art pop, r&b soul, indie pop)
Simulation Swarm — 13 plays (indie, folk, alternative)
What You Like — 9 plays (electronic, house, dance)

So instead of charting everything, I turned each one into a color mode you can switch using SPACE, kind of like my own Spotify visualizer. I kept the layout minimal and centered like Spotify's. I wanted to make something that I could see in the app as an emotional waveform view.

From class, I used:

  • let variables to control animation speed, sound levels, and state
  • for loops to draw multiple wave rows instead of repeating code manually
  • constrain() to make sure the waves stayed inside the frame
  • Interaction functions like mousePressed() and keyPressed() so the viewer can pause or swap moods

Some other of the functions I learned off YouTube. I've been really invested in sound and how it interacts; in my Major Studio I did a project on Touch Designer and it kind of mirrors this one. I wanted to explore how sound and mood overlap, and it was interesting to think about rhythm in a visual way rather than just as audio. I can see myself using this system in future projects to track emotions through movement instead of just numbers like traditional Spotify stats.

About

I'm Pilar Liotta — an Argentine visual designer currently based in New York. My work sits at the intersection of branding, editorial, and creative code. I like to blend color, typography, and motion to tell personal and commercial stories.

I studied at Parsons School of Design where I focused on experimental typography, interaction design, and creative computation. My practice ranges from packaging systems and printed matter to p5.js experiments and short motion pieces.

I'm interested in projects that mix craft and computation, and I enjoy collaborating across disciplines — designers, coders, photographers, and directors.
